Okay, here's a tutorial :

PART ONE: DRAWING

NOTES:If you already have an object you wanted to use, you do not need to draw a new one!
The DRAWING tutorial only works for SIMPLE SHAPES!!

First, click on the rectangle tool and drag it. You should see a row of shapes. For the logo thing, I used the almost-circular shape.
Go to the main drawing area and drag the shape to draw it.

Click on the Fill tool and click inside the shape you have drawn. This fills it with a certain colour of your choice.
Then, click on the Selection Tool and use it to select your shape.

Above the row of colours at the bottom of the screen, there should be a box called Line.
To remove the line, type 0 into it and click Apply at the left-hand side of the screen.
To make it thinner or thicker, type in a number.
Don't forget to click Apply!

PART TWO: FADING

This is a bit more complex. If you want to make multiple shapes fade, select them all by either shift-clicking on them one by one or by pressing Ctrl-A (If you made an object the background e.g. used a large black rectangle as I did, make sure the objects you want to select are on another layer! To make another layer, click the first of 3 small yellow buttons below the timeline at the left hand side.)

Once you have selected your object(s), click on Insert and then on "Transform Motion". Depending on how long you want the fade in to last for, drag the second blue dot on the timeline to the left or right, or just leave it alone.

Then select the frame that the FIRST blue dot is on by clicking above it.
Select the shape(s) and beside the colour bar, there should be a box saying "None". Click on it and select Alpha. It should be at 100%. If not, type it in. Then click Apply. It should be in the same place when you removed/changed the line of your shape.

To fade out, click above the SECOND blue dot.

To fade in AND out, do the first part of the Fading tutorial , then after clicking above the SECOND blue dot, select the object(s) and repeat the Fading tutorial

BUT

Click above the SECOND BLUE DOT OF THE SECOND BLUE LINE, select the thing(s) you want to fade and click on the box that says "None" at the bottom of the screen and choose Alpha. Don't forget to make the Alpha 100% and to click Apply!!

Follow this tutorial and you too can have a Flash with fading effects!

I was going to call this a mini-tutorial, but there's nothing mini about it!

How many times have you gone to a computer lab at work or at school or even the library and have not been able to use your favorite browser? (Assuming it is FireFox... ) You may have thought to yourself, "how shall I cope, how will I survive in this crazy world of... *gasp* Microsoft Internet Explorer!?!?!?" Well, never fear! BenjaminJ is here to help you figure out how to get back online the FireFox way (+ flash! which is not as easy as it may sound).

First, the issue:
Many computer labs do not have FireFox installed and if they do they have an old version.
These computers also sometimes fail to have the plugin for flash available to FireFox.
These computers are "Locked Down" to restrict any non-administrator from installing any programs.

Solution:
When you log on, go to getfirefox.com and click on the download link. If you are able to download, we are well on our way.
Run the install and have it install not to the "Program Files" directory, but instead to a different folder such as Desktop\Firefox or C:\downloads\FireFox.
Now to install the flash plugin... you need to have access to a computer that has FireFox and the firefox flash plugin installed...
Go to the firefox plugins directory and copy it to a USB drive (or, make it a zip file and put it on a floppy disk).
    (Actually, all you need are the files in the plugins directory that pertain to flash. Simple inspection of the filenames should get you well on your way to being able to pick those files out, but here is the list I made from my computer which has Flash Player 9 installed: flashplayer.xpt, getflash.exe, getflash.exe.manifest, np32dsw.dll, npswf32.dll, npswf32_flashutil.exe, shockwaveplugin.class.)

Now, copy these files over to the other computer, and you should be good to go!

Note: your school/whatever lab may have rules about whether you are allowed to install stuff or not. You should (*cough* probably *cough*) follow these rules at all times. You are not permitted to hold me responsible for the consequences of any actions you take based on the instructions in this tutorial. Also, just because this worked for me does not mean it will work for you. System administrators are able to lock systems down in more ways than I could possibly imagine, much less think of ways to get around. Use this tutorial at your own risk.

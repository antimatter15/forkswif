Ok, let's see if I can make this as simple as possible.

EDITED FOR NEW EASIER CONTENT

Open Liveswif and create another layer. You will want to have at least a couple to start, or if you already have a movie you wish to use, you will want a couple other layers to hold two more sprites you are about to create.

You will now create a new sprite, call it holder. For this sprite, you will create a small circle in the center of the stage, and enter this in the actionscript area:
Quote:
_root._x = -_root.cam._x*(500 /_root.cam._width);
_root._y = -_root.cam._y*(400 /_root.cam._height);
_root._xscale = 100 *(500 /_root.cam._width);
_root._yscale = 100 *(400 /_root.cam._height);

Replace 400 with your own stage height and 500 with your own stage width. The defaults are 400 by 400. Then go to the next frame (frame 2) and enter the same actionscript again for your sprite. You don't have to make another object, just paste in the actionscript.

Next step, create another new sprite. You will want it to be a box the same size as the stage, and make it filled with white, 25 or 75 opacity. (i.e. make it kinda transparent, but enough so you can still see it). Also in your sprite, make a smallish circle in the exact middle for a viewfinder. In the actionscript for the sprite, put

Quote:
_visible = false;


This will make your "camera" disappear when you export the swf.

Now your sprites are done.

Go back to your main scene and choose one of your layers. In this layer, put in your holder sprite somewhere off-stage and use the transform tween to make it persist to the end of your movie. Next choose your other layer and then place an instance of your cam on the stage. Call the "instance name" "cam".

That's pretty much it. Now you can finish your movie, just be sure to keep the layer with the "cam" in it on top so you can see it as what is contained within this box will be the only visible part of the movie.

You can resize the cam and also distort the picture. Since it is a virtual camera, when you make the camera box wider, it will make the objects skinnier. Inversely if you make it more narrow, the objects will become wider. I usually think of it as a piece of gum or balloon that has to be stretched or squeezed to fit the viewing width and height. You'll have to play with it yourself to see what I mean.

example:

http://www.stratt.com/swf/distort.swf

By far, the easiest thing to do though is to use the transform motion to tween the camera in, out and around the stage as illustrated here:

http://www.stratt.com/swf/panzoom.html

There are some limitations using this implementation. The rotation doesn't quite work on the camera, nor does the stretch skew, but it sure beats trying to tween a bunch of layers to make them all appear to zoom and pan at the same relative speed.

Enjoy.

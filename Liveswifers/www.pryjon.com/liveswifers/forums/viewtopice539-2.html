GIMP 2.2 to the rescue! For your white box problem, here is what you do:

open the image you want to remove the whitespace from
select the layer->transparency->color to alpha... option on the image's menu bar.
the default color in the color preview button should be white; if it isn't, make it so. Then click OK.
Save as .gif OR .png (PNG is better)

Problem: Aaaah! Now the eyes/paper/other white things that isn't whitespace is see-through! What do I do?
Answer: You have to do some extra steps, here they are:

open the image you want to remove the whitespace from
fill ALL the extra whitespace with a flat (non-gradient) color that doesn't appear in the rest of your image.
select the layer->transparency->color to alpha... option on the image's menu bar.
set the color in the color preview button to the color you used to fill in the whitespace and click OK.
Save as .gif OR .png (PNG is better)
Don't fill in the whitespace with a complicated color, such as #ABC123 (produces a darkish yellow-green), use a simple color like red or blue to fill in the whitespace. It'll be easier.

May be a little hard to follow, but I tried my best!

PNG's need an alpha channel in order to have transparency. This means that they can have variable transparency on each pixel. Gif's have one color of transparency. It order for the transparency to work in PNG's, the pixel has to be set as transparent, not just white.

transparency and alpha channels are the same in the sense that if something is 100% transparent, it will have an alpha value of 0. If something is 0% transparent (opaque) it will have an alpha value of 255. 256 levels of transparency are available in PNG's, and 2 levels (on and off) are available in GIF files. alpha values are stored in the alpha channel, just like the amount of green, red, or blue in a pixel is stored in the green, red, or blue channel, respectively.

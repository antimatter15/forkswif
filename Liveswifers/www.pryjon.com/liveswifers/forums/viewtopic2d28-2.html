Yes, you do need code to make the button work properly.

In the Actionscript box (it looks like a normal text box, near the bottom of the screen), type in:

Code:
gotoAndPlay(*insert frame number here*);


...where *insert frame number here* is the frame number you want to go to.
(Make sure the Button Action is On Press).

Deselect the button, by clicking away from it and then in the Actionscript area type in:

Code:
stop();


(Make sure the Frame Action is On Frame).

You should have a working button.

Somewhere around here is my button tutorial (which I originally made for the old forums on liveswif.com)... I can't find it, but I didn't look very hard, ha ha. Here is the repost (not to be confused with 'repast'! Ha ha... nvm, vocabulary joke):

BenjaminJ wrote:
To create a button:
[Insert] > [Button]
In the Timeline Window you will see a few layers:
- Hit State
- Down State
- Over State
- Up State

HitState must contain the area of the button that is clickable. It doesn't need to be the right color or anything: it won't show up. It just needs to be the right shape.

The UpState will be the normal look of the button when the mouse is not over it.

The OverState is... You guessed it! The way the button will look when the mouse is over the area covered in the HitState. (Note, if you use a shape in the hitstate and forget to fill it in, the area covered is only the OUTLINE of the shape. You must fill it in with a color. Any color will do because it won't show up.)

The DownState will only show up if your actionscript is not set to On Press; it shows up while the mouse is clicked (or held down).


When you are done making your button, click back to your main scene by clicking [Scene 1] (right underneath the Timeline Window).

Now, in the Library Window (on the right) click Library, if it isn't clicked already, and you will see the button you just made. Drag it to the main scene and place whereever you like. (Note: if you want the button to last more than one frame you should probably place it on its own layer, click it, click [Insert] > [Transform Motion (F6)] and adjust/slide that last blue dot in that layer on the timeline window.)

Before adding any actionscript, preview the movie ([File] > [Preview (F12)]). Try putting your mouse over the button and clicking it. Is it the way you want it?

If it looks good, close the preview window, and click your button again look at the pop-down box to see which event you want. "On Press" or "On Release" are the one you most likely want to use. Now add the actionscript in the big white textbox.

Here are some sample scripts in case you (or someone else browsing the forum) need(s) it:

Code:
stop();

Stops the movie.
Code:
play();

Plays the movie.
Code:
gotoAndStop("x");

Goes to frame "x" and stops. Use frame number in place of x.
Code:
gotoAndPlay("x");

Goes to frame "x" and plays.
Code:
getURL("http://www.yoursite.url/folder/file.ext", "_blank");

Sends the browser to the address specified in the specified frame. "_blank" opens a new window; omiting everything after the comma opens in the same window (if you are aready in your browser, otherwise it opens a new window).

(Note: if you fail to add the actionscript, all your buttons will do is blink.)

Now that you have a button finish your movie and export to SWF. Save in the same folder as the webpage. To start with "My Documents" is fine. Close or minimize LiveSwif, and open the webpage you are using (using your favorite text editor or webpage editor making sure you are in "Code View" or "Source View"). Insert the following code:

Code:
<cobject classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000"
codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=4,0,2,0" width="120" height="50">
   <param name="BASE" value="">
   <param name=quality value=High>
   <param name="BGCOLOR" value="">
   <param name="Src" value="button1.swf">
   <param name="Play" value="-1">
   <param name="Loop" value="0">

      <embed src="button1.swf" quality=high pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/shockwave/download/index.cgi?P1_Prod_Version=ShockwaveFlash" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="120" height="50" bgcolor="" base="." loop="0">
    </embed></cobject>


Where "button1.swf" is the name of your exported LiveSwif, 120 is the height, and 50 is the width. Reset these to whatever you need. (Note: By setting loop to "0" your movie will stop on the last frame. Remove if you don't want this.)

Now save your webpage in the same folder as your .swf file, and open in you browser (Internet Explorer, Firefox, whatever.) to see if it appears as you want. (If you use a different folder, make sure you specify that in 'src' parameters.

Good Luck!

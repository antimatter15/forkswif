Get the regular version. The Lite version was a demo that came out before the regular version when Liveswif was still being sold.
The "new version" you're referring to is Openswif, which is still in development.

Mercedes wrote:
Note, I have never used Flash before nor Action script. I am however familiar with VBS...and a bit of html..I can read code well....

I like to think of Liveswif as a program that lets you get started with Flash/Digital Animation without having to shell out for it. Use it to get the hang of animation and vector drawing until you're ready to move onto something a little more professional.

Yes, some people have provided their files to look at, which you may look at for an example. There are also tutorial around here somewhere...oom...yes there right here, in the tutorial section, where most codes are pasted.

Flash is probably the most popular animation format, it can be found anywhere from adverts to games to movies to banners to applications to a whole Flash website.

LiveSwif produces Flash, which can be published in many sites!
If you see Flash Games at a game site, I'm sure you can host them where people might give it a look often if it's a little popular.

DeviantArt is where people do share their artwork, from Flash to painting to digital art.

Newgrounds is an immensely popular website about Flash Movies, Games, and Music, but it takes a good Flash to be hosted on there.

Kongregate is a smaller website about Flash Games, produced by regular people like you and me. If you manage to make a popular game here you can earn a little pocket money.

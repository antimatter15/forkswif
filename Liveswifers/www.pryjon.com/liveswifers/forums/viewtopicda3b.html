It depends on what version of Flash you're comparing Liveswif to.

Liveswif is more like Flash 5 in terms of its Actionscript (AS 1.0), so AS 2 and 3 won't work in it. You also can't have filters, a paintbrush tool or shape tweens (which is no big loss considering most shape tweens look terrible).

Flash does tend to have more features, but it is from the creators of the Flash format and is really expensive (somewhere in the area of several hundred dollars for the Pro version, which has all Flash features), while Liveswif is a freeware program made by someone who isn't hired by Adobe/Macromedia.

$700 - $800 for the new Flash 9 (CS3)

Liveswif is not the same as flash, the creator(s) probaly wern't hired by the actual Flash Creator: Macromedia (bought out by Adobe).

The creators had crate Liveswif as an inexpensive alternative to the more expensive $400 Flash 5 (at the time of creation)

It used to be $40, I believe until they made it free.

---

Liveswif has many of the core features, like Movie Clips, Graphics (Sprites in Liveswif), Buttons and such.

I believe Liveswif has an easier to learn interface. Making buttons is easier, and Movie Clips and Graphics are called sprites, the AS syntax is already included in the box. The color editor is easier to me, and much more.

The only con is that Liveswif dosen't have support for the later versions of AS, easier to make text boxes, masking.

But many have found alternatives to these setbacks.


End
